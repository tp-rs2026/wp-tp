2. How Payments Work

Tapara supports the following payment methods:

2.1 Bank Payments (Akahu)

When you pre-select your bank in the Tapara app, payments are processed as one-off bank-to-bank transfers via Akahu, a CDR-accredited open banking intermediary. You will be redirected to Akahu’s hosted page to authenticate with your bank and authorise each payment. Funds transfer directly from your bank account to the merchant’s bank account. Tapara does not intermediate or hold funds in this process. There are no transaction fees for bank payments.

By using this payment method, you acknowledge that you are also subject to Akahu’s End User Terms and the terms and conditions of your bank.

2.2 Card Payments (Stripe)

If you save a payment card in the Tapara app, card payments are processed by Stripe, an authorised payment processor. Off-session charges are made against your saved card when you approve a payment. Card transaction fees may apply and are the merchant’s responsibility. The merchant may choose to pass card processing costs to you as a surcharge line item on your bill.

By saving a card, you acknowledge that you are also subject to Stripe’s Terms of Service.

2.3 Card-Present Payments (Tap to Pay)

For in-person payments where you do not have the Tapara app available, the merchant may accept payment by having you tap your physical card or device on their phone. These payments are processed by Stripe Terminal. Card-present transaction fees apply.

2.4 Cash

Merchants may accept cash payments and record them through the Tapara app. No digital funds movement occurs for cash transactions.

6. Refunds and Disputes

Tapara does not sell goods or services and does not hold your funds. Refund and dispute processes depend on the payment method used:

  • Bank payments (Akahu): Bank-to-bank transfers cannot be reversed programmatically. If you require a refund for a bank payment, you must resolve this directly with the merchant. The merchant may issue a refund via their own bank transfer.
  • Card payments (Stripe): Refunds for card payments are processed through Stripe’s refund system. The merchant initiates the refund and Stripe handles the reversal to your card.
  • Cash: Cash refunds are handled directly between you and the merchant.

For all payment disputes, you should first contact the merchant directly. If you are unable to resolve the issue with the merchant, you may contact us at support@tapara.co.nz.
