Merchant Agreement

Effective date: 11 June 2026 · Last updated: 11 June 2026

This Merchant Agreement (“Agreement”) is between Tapara Limited (NZBN 9429053591470, FSP1012381) (“Tapara”, “we”, “us”, “our”) and the business owner (“you”, “Merchant”) who registers a business on the Tapara platform.

By creating a business on Tapara and accepting this Agreement, you agree to be bound by its terms. Please also review our Privacy Policy and Complaints Process.

1. The Tapara Platform

Tapara provides a mobile platform that enables merchants to accept payments from consumers via the Tapara app, manage customer loyalty programs, and access customer analytics. The platform includes NFC-based customer check-in, an order builder, payment request flows, and loyalty program management tools.

Tapara is not a bank or payment processor. We provide a technology platform that facilitates payment instructions between consumers, merchants, and third-party payment providers. Tapara does not hold, receive, or process any funds when facilitating a transaction.

2. Payment Processing

2.1 Payment Methods

Tapara supports the following payment methods for merchant transactions:

  • Bank payments (Akahu): Consumer-to-merchant bank-to-bank transfers via Akahu open banking. Funds transfer directly from the consumer’s bank account to your bank account. Tapara does not intermediate funds.
  • Saved card (Stripe): Off-session card charges via Stripe Connect. Payments are processed as destination charges to your Stripe connected account.
  • Tap to Pay (Stripe Terminal): Card-present contactless payments where the consumer taps their physical card or device on your phone. Processed via Stripe Terminal as direct charges on your connected account.
  • Cash: You confirm cash receipt in the app. No digital funds movement occurs.

2.2 Third-Party Payment Providers

To accept payments through Tapara, you must comply with the terms and conditions of the relevant payment providers:

  • Akahu: Bank payments are facilitated by Akahu Technologies Limited, a CDR-accredited open banking intermediary. You are subject to Akahu’s terms for payment processing.
  • Stripe: Card payments (saved card and Tap to Pay) are processed through Stripe Connect. You will be onboarded as a Stripe Express connected account. You are subject to Stripe’s Connected Account Agreement and must complete Stripe’s identity verification process.

2.3 Settlement

  • Bank payments (Akahu): Funds settle directly into your nominated bank account via the bank transfer. Settlement timing depends on your bank’s processing schedule.
  • Card payments (Stripe): Funds settle to your Stripe connected account and are paid out to your bank account according to Stripe’s standard payout schedule for New Zealand.
  • Cash: No settlement through Tapara. Cash is received directly by you.

3. Fees

3.1 Transaction Fees

  • Bank payments (Akahu): 0% transaction fee at launch. Tapara reserves the right to introduce transaction fees for bank payments in the future, with at least 30 days’ notice.
  • Saved card (Stripe): Standard Stripe NZ pricing applies — currently 2.65% + $0.30 NZD per domestic transaction. International and currency conversion rates may differ. These fees are your responsibility as the merchant.
  • Tap to Pay (Stripe Terminal): Standard Stripe NZ pricing applies — currently 2.6% + $0.20 NZD per domestic card-present transaction.
  • Cash: No fees.

You may optionally add a surcharge line item to the bill to pass card processing costs to the consumer, subject to applicable New Zealand law.

3.2 Platform Subscription

After an initial free trial period, merchants subscribe to Tapara on a volume-based monthly plan. Tier allocation is based on payment volume processed through Tapara. All features, including loyalty program tools, are available on every plan.

Subscription pricing will be communicated before the free trial period ends. You may cancel your subscription at any time, subject to the termination provisions in section 11 of this Agreement.

4. Merchant Responsibilities

As a merchant on Tapara, you agree to:

  • Use the Services only for genuine and lawful transactions of goods or services where you are the actual provider.
  • Provide accurate and complete business information, including your business name, category, and bank account details.
  • Keep your bank account details and business information current and accurate.
  • Set accurate prices for items in the order builder and ensure that payment request amounts reflect the actual goods or services provided.
  • Handle customer service, including responding to customer inquiries and complaints about your goods or services.
  • Process refunds in a timely manner where required:
    • For Stripe card transactions, initiate refunds through the Tapara app (processed via Stripe’s refund system).
    • For Akahu bank transactions, process refunds directly via your own bank transfer to the consumer.
    • For cash transactions, handle refunds directly with the consumer.
  • Comply with all applicable New Zealand laws, including tax obligations (GST where applicable), the Consumer Guarantees Act 1993, the Fair Trading Act 1986, and any applicable regulations regarding payment surcharges.
  • Not use the Services for any prohibited or restricted business categories as notified by Tapara or required by our payment providers.

5. Bank Account and Identity

You are required to provide a valid New Zealand bank account number to receive payments via bank transfer. By providing your bank account details, you confirm that you are the authorised holder of that account.

Tapara may be required to verify your identity or business details as part of our obligations under the AML/CFT Act. You agree to provide any additional information reasonably requested for this purpose.

6. Loyalty Programs

Tapara provides tools for you to create and manage your own loyalty program. You design your program’s tier names, progression rules, and benefits. Tapara provides the infrastructure, consumer app surface, and automatic tier progression.

  • You are responsible for the content and delivery of your loyalty program, including any benefits you offer.
  • Structured benefits (percentage discounts, fixed discounts, free item vouchers) are applied automatically by the Tapara platform based on your configured rules.
  • Custom benefits (e.g. priority seating, early access) are your responsibility to deliver in-store.
  • You may modify or discontinue your loyalty program at any time. Changes take effect for future transactions.
  • Consumer membership data (visit count, spend, tier status) is accessible to you through the Tapara platform.

7. Data and Privacy

Your use of the Services is subject to our Privacy Policy. You acknowledge that:

  • Tapara collects and processes transaction data, business information, and customer interaction data as described in our Privacy Policy.
  • Consumer personal information (name, tier status, visit history) is shared with you through the platform for the purpose of facilitating the merchant-consumer relationship. You must handle this information in accordance with the Privacy Act 2020.
  • Tapara does not sell personal information to third parties.

9. Permitted Use

You may only use the Services for genuine and lawful transactions of goods or services. You must not:

  • Use the Services for transactions where you are not the actual provider of the goods or services.
  • Use the Services for money laundering, terrorist financing, or any other unlawful purpose.
  • Attempt to circumvent payment limits, transaction monitoring, or other security measures.
  • Access, interfere with, or disrupt the Services, other users’ accounts, or the underlying infrastructure.
  • Share your account credentials or allow unauthorised persons to access your merchant account.

10. Suspension

Tapara may suspend or restrict your access to the Services at any time if:

  • You breach this Agreement or any applicable law.
  • We reasonably suspect fraudulent, unlawful, or suspicious activity on your account.
  • We are required to do so by law, regulation, or a government agency.
  • Your Stripe connected account is suspended or restricted by Stripe.
  • Your actions pose a risk to Tapara, other users, or the integrity of the platform.
  • You fail to pay any subscription fees when due.

We will notify you of any suspension where we are legally permitted to do so.

11. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement:

  • You may close your business on Tapara at any time from the Admin tab in the app.
  • Tapara may terminate this Agreement with 30 days’ written notice, or immediately if you breach this Agreement, engage in unlawful activity, or your Stripe account is terminated.

Upon termination:

  • You must cease accepting transactions through Tapara.
  • Any outstanding subscription fees remain payable.
  • Stripe will process any remaining payouts to your bank account according to their standard schedule.
  • Transaction records will be retained for 7 years as required by the AML/CFT Act.

You agree to indemnify and hold harmless Tapara Limited, its directors, employees, and agents from any claims, losses, damages, liabilities, and expenses (including legal fees) arising from:

14. Dispute Resolution

If a dispute arises between you and Tapara:

  1. Good faith resolution: Both parties will attempt to resolve the dispute through good faith discussion within 10 business days.
  2. Escalation: If the dispute cannot be resolved, either party may refer it to the Financial Dispute Resolution Service (FDRS) at fdrs.org.nz or by calling 0508 337 337. The FDRS provides a free, independent dispute resolution service.
  3. Legal proceedings: If the dispute cannot be resolved through the FDRS, either party may commence legal proceedings in the courts of New Zealand.

We may update this Agreement from time to time. Where changes are material, we will provide at least 14 days’ notice via the app or email before the changes take effect. Changes required for security or legal compliance reasons may take effect immediately.

Your continued use of the Services after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated Agreement. If you do not agree with the changes, you may terminate this Agreement as described in section 11.
